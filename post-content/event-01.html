About the Event

The OutKept Phishathon 2025, organised by OutKept and Savaco, was a hands-on social engineering competition that pushed participants well beyond typical lab exercises. Unlike most cybersecurity events that stay theoretical, this one dropped us straight into realistic phishing simulations — including a public-figure scenario and a deepfake task that required careful planning, ethical consideration, and clean execution.

What Was Covered

The event was structured around targeted phishing simulations of increasing complexity. Each scenario demanded a different approach: researching the target, building a convincing pretext, and executing without relying on cheap tricks. The deepfake challenge in particular stood out — it was not just a technical task but a question of where the line sits between a realistic simulation and an ethical overstep.

My Takeaway

The biggest lesson I took away was about method. In cybersecurity, and especially in social engineering, the quality of your research and the strength of your pretext matter far more than any flashy tool or technique. A well-constructed, targeted approach will outperform a generic attack every time. That is true in competitions and it is true in real penetration testing engagements.

Research, pretext quality, and a targeted approach matter more than flashy tricks.

Verdict

This was one of the most practical and eye-opening events I attended this year. It directly reinforced what we study in the cybersecurity programme at Howest — but in a way that no classroom exercise can fully replicate. I would attend again without hesitation and would strongly recommend it to any cybersecurity student who wants to understand social engineering from the inside out.
