About the Event

This Tech & Meet session, organised by Howest and led by Nico Declerck, focused on a topic that has been looming over the networking world for years: the transition from IPv4 to IPv6. IPv4 exhaustion is no longer a theoretical future problem — it is happening now, and organisations that have been putting off their migration are running out of time and address space.

What Was Covered

Nico walked us through why the exhaustion problem is real and urgent, what dual-stack deployments actually look like in practice, and the full range of considerations organisations face when planning a migration: addressing strategy, DNS configuration, security policy updates, and monitoring. The session did not shy away from the complexity — it was honest about the fact that a proper IPv6 migration touches almost every layer of an organisation's infrastructure.

My Takeaway

From a cybersecurity perspective, IPv6 introduces attack surfaces and misconfigurations that many security teams are not yet fully prepared for. A lot of existing firewall rules, intrusion detection signatures, and monitoring tools were built with IPv4 in mind. Working with IPv6 securely is not just about understanding the protocol — it requires revisiting your entire security posture. This session made that very concrete for me.

The future is here when it comes to IPv6. Working with it securely is just as important as understanding how it works.

Verdict

A well-structured, technically honest session that covered ground directly relevant to network security. Nico Declerck clearly has deep practical experience with this topic and it showed in the quality of the examples and the answers during Q&A. I would attend future Tech & Meet sessions organised by Howest without hesitation.
