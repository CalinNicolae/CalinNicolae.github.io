About the Event

.NET 10 Demystified was a technical session organised in collaboration with Howest, bringing together developers, architects, and engineers to explore where the .NET ecosystem is heading and what that means for day-to-day engineering work. The event had a practical focus throughout — less about announcements, more about what actually changes when you sit down to write and ship code.

What Was Covered

The session covered the latest .NET 10 features in depth, with a strong emphasis on how to apply them in real-world projects rather than toy examples. Topics included performance tuning, building maintainable APIs, and designing cloud-ready architectures. The speakers drew heavily on their own production experience, which made the content immediately useful rather than purely academic.

My Takeaway

As a cybersecurity student, I came at this from a slightly different angle than most attendees, I was less focused on the framework features and more interested in the security implications of the architectural patterns being discussed. Maintainable APIs and cloud-ready systems are exactly the kind of infrastructure I will need to assess and secure in my career. Understanding how developers build them makes me a better tester. The networking aspect was also valuable — talking to architects and senior developers gave me a clearer picture of how software teams actually operate.

Verdict

Even for someone whose primary focus is security rather than development, this event delivered real value. It is a good reminder that cybersecurity does not exist in isolation — understanding the tools and patterns developers use every day is part of the job. I would recommend it to any student who wants to build a more complete picture of the software landscape they will be working in.
