About the Event

This Tech & Meet session was focused on DeepSeek and its newly unveiled advancements in AI. The expert speaker, Dimitri Casier, guided the session through how next-generation models are pushing the boundaries of what is possible — not just in raw performance, but in accessibility, efficiency, and responsible development.

What Was Covered

The session explored how DeepSeek's approach is reshaping parts of the AI landscape through a focus on compute efficiency and openness. Dimitri covered how optimization is becoming a central pillar of modern AI research — the question is no longer only what a model can do, but how efficiently it can do it and who can access it. The discussion also touched on responsible AI development and what transparency actually means in practice for large model releases.

My Takeaway

As someone in cybersecurity, AI developments like this matter to me on two levels. The first is the obvious one: AI capabilities are advancing faster than most organisations can track, and that creates new attack surfaces and new tools for both defenders and attackers. The second is subtler: the conversation around efficiency and accessibility signals that powerful AI is becoming available to more actors, not fewer. That shift has real implications for how we think about threat modelling and defence strategy going forward.

AI capabilities are evolving faster than ever — and the conversation is shifting from raw performance to efficiency, accessibility, and responsibility.

Verdict

A genuinely interesting session that went beyond surface-level AI hype and engaged with the practical and strategic implications of these developments. Dimitri Casier brought clear expertise and made space for good discussion. Events like this are exactly why I value the Tech & Meet format — they connect what is happening in industry to what we are learning in our studies, and they do it with people who are actually working in the field.
